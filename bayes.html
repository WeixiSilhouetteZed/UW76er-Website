Introduction to Bayesian Statistics

Over the course of undergraduate study at the University of Waterloo, I found that there is not a single statistics course on the Bayesian method (some of them provide partial introduction). So, we are basically a school of frequentists. This is not a set of diss tracks against the frequentist approach as that would be comparing apples to bananas. Providing a new perspective on an intuitive level is the main goal of this endeavour.

Disclaimer: The materials presented here are merely my summary work on a set of amazing YouTube videos created by ox educ. Their videos, created in 2014, are definitely of high quality and hidden gems just like a lot of other math/statistics series.

Moreover, some of the examples are found in some notable texts that you will find references as you read. I will try to keep it interesting as everything will be in the first-person narrative and I do not intend to write a dry textbook.

Prerequisites: any introductory course on probability theory and preferably also a course in basic statistics (frequentist version). At the University of Waterloo, those would be STAT231 and STAT230/330. Basically, make sure you know what PMF/PDF, CDF, and maximum likelihood methods mean. If you don't, there is no need to find another textbook or wait for another course, just go onto your local inter-web and Google those concepts.

Motivation

Who came up with this? To be honest, I don't care that much. But the name Bayesian certainly makes one think it is related to the Bayes rule that you probably learned in your first-year probability theory. If you don't recall, just consider the following problem, definitely a classic one.

Problem - Rainy/Sunny Suppose the city of Toronto has the following climate scheme. If the Raptors win a game, then the day is sunny for 90% of the time and rainy for the rest of 10% of the time. If the Raptors lose a game, then the day is sunny for 10% of the time. The probability of the Raptors winning a game is 60%. What is the probability of the Raptors winning a game given that the day is rainy?
